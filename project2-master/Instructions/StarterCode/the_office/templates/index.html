Insights

    Our analysis was geared towards showing the evolution of the show through the changing popularity of its' characters. Obviously the show was a great success, but we wanted to see which characters contributed more or less to the overall popularity.
    The pie chart shows the amount of lines spoken by the character as a % of total script. The gauge shows the episode rating, going from a gray (0) to Dwight's mustard shirt color (10). Both of the illustration tell the story of both character and user popularity as the application user can track the evolution of both.
    Our observations have confirmed the overwhelming popularity of Michael Scott (played by Steve Carell). Average rating for episodes with Michael were 8.42, as opposed to 7.78 without. The highest rated episode is the last one (923) coming in at 9.8/10.
    Michael has the highest number of overall lines even though he was missing from 49 episodes. Highest number of lines are spoken by Micheal, Dwight and Jim made up 20.2%, 12.6% and 11.3% of total lines, respectively.
    Jim Halpert (played by John Krasinsky) and Dwight Schrute (played by Rainn Wilson) are the only two characters who were present in every episode of The Office aired.
